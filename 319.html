1934 BMW 319

“In the 1930s, BMW was having such great racing success with the 315 roadster that it decided to produce a version for the public. The 319 followed a similar path: highly successful racing roadster turned streetcar. Although the 319’s body styling was very similar to the 315 and both cars were produced at the same time, the 319 sported a more powerful inline 6-cylinder engine with 45 horsepower, reaching a top speed of 72 mph.”

“The 319 was produced in five body versions, including 3,029 of this particular saloon/sedan. The racing success of the 315 and 319 helped increase BMW's market penetration, so although the powerful 319 was only produced from 1934 to 1937, it helped BMW make a name for itself.”
