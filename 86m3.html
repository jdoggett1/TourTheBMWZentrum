1986 BMW M3

“Since its Wheels first officially touch the track in 1986, the M3 has made an art of proving BMW’s ‘Ultimate Driving Machine’ tagline true. The powerful performance car, which has one of the world's most loyal followings, was not originally intended to be mass-produced. Built on the 3 Series, the M3 was developed so that it could be raced as a touring car.”

“The revolutionary result, however—an elegant, aerodynamic monster that married high-performance motorsport technology with everyday practicality—was a joy to drive and quickly became a flagship sports car for BMW. The first M3 featured a 4-cylinder engine that delivered 200 horsepower and swept the competition; today, the M3 Series continues to thrill drivers across the globe.”
