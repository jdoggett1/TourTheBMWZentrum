1930 Ihle DIXI Roadster

“Iconic BMW design owes a great deal to the Ihle DIXI, a modified racing roadster that was produced from 1930 to 1933. This automobile—only the second BMW model ever to be made—pioneered the kidney-shaped twin radiator grill that became signature to BMW design.”

“Introduced during a famous automobile race, the Ihle DIXI was modified by the Ihle Brothers to include an improved carburetor, transmission and brake system for better handling speed. The 4-cylinder engine delivered 15 horsepower and top speed of approximately 50 mph. Ihle DIXI gave rise to newer BMWs, but its contribution to BMW’s design DNA has lasted for decades.”
