1999 BMW Z8 Roadster

“The Z1 was named after ‘zukunft,’ the German word for ‘future.’ The name proved apt because the sleek, 2-seater roadster did more than offer a glimpse of the future. It invented it. The Z1 was conceived as a pilot project for BMW's new think tank, meant to develop innovative designs and development processes.”

“However, the result was so stunning the car was green-lined for limited production. The Z1 featured a 2.5-liter inline 6-cylinder engine with 168 horsepower, along with several innovations, including vertical sliding doors, aerodynamic styling and a body made entirely of thermoplastics—the first car ever designed to be recycled. Eight thousand Z1s were produced from 1989 to 1991, but the technology has advanced in its creation pioneered new ways of designing and building BMWs.”
