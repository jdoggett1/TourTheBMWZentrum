1999 BMW Z8 Roadster

“Built for speed, the legendary BMW Z8 roadster was only in production for a short 3 years—but that didn't stop it from raising the bar, charming collectors and inspiring future design. The car launched in 1999 as an exclusive, short-run series and quickly claimed global attention when it was prominently featured in a James Bond film. Directly inspired by BMW's 1950s-era 507 roadster, the Z8’s sleek design imagined how the 507 might have evolved over the years."

"Beloved for its unique combination of sensuous, mid-century styling and supercar-like power, the Z8 sported a 5-liter V8 engine that delivered 394 horsepower and a 0-60 mile per hour time of 4.5 seconds. Only 5,703 Z8s were produced, but the car’s impact on auto design can still be felt today.”
